INSIGHTS
▲
95.7% Year-1 Supply Inflation — Extreme Dilution Risk — Supply nearly doubles in first 4 quarters
▾
The spreadsheet reveals that circulating supply grows from 227.8M tokens at TGE (Q1) to 445.7M by end of year 1 (Q4) — a 95.7% increase in just four quarters. This is driven primarily by Seed tokens unlocking at 20M/quarter, KOL Sale distributing ~4.2M/quarter, Pre-Sale completing by Q7, and Community releasing ~18–22M/quarter. For any token to sustain its price under this level of new supply entering the market, it would need an extraordinary rate of new demand — user adoption, staking lockups, and platform transaction growth all have to outpace a near-doubling of circulating tokens in the first year alone. This is one of the most significant risk factors in the entire tokenomics design and will require active demand-side management at launch.
▲
KOL Sale 49.1% Unlocked at TGE — Heavy Day-1 Sell Pressure — 44.2M tokens immediately liquid
▾
The KOL (Key Opinion Leader) allocation of 90M tokens (9% of total supply) begins at 44.17M unlocked at TGE — 49.1% of the full KOL allocation available on day one. This is a structurally aggressive TGE unlock for KOLs, who are by definition promotional participants with high-ROI incentive to sell early. Combined with the Seed allocation unlocking 30M at TGE (20% of Seed), total day-one insider-adjacent liquidity is 82.3M tokens, representing 36.1% of the entire Q1 circulating supply. Projects with high KOL TGE unlocks routinely experience sharp post-listing selloffs as influencers take profits, undermining retail buyer confidence. A cliff-then-linear structure for KOLs (common in better-designed launches) would have been preferable.
▲
Seed Investors: 20% Unlocked at TGE With No Cliff — 30M tokens liquid immediately, linear through Q7
▾
Seed investors (15% of total supply, 150M tokens) receive 30M tokens at TGE — a 20% immediate unlock with no cliff period. The remainder unlocks linearly at approximately 20M tokens per quarter, with full unlock by Q7 (under 2 years). Industry best practice for seed investors typically requires a 6–12 month cliff followed by 18–36 months of linear vesting. The absence of a cliff means seed investors face no lock-up incentive to stay supportive during the critical post-launch period. Their average ROI multiple at listing is undisclosed (no round price published), but at any listing price above seed cost, these investors are in profit immediately and have economic incentive to sell into early market demand. This creates structural sell pressure precisely when the token most needs price stability.
▲
Listing Price & FDV Undisclosed — No Valuation Anchor — Cannot assess entry risk or peer benchmarking
▾
No listing price or FDV has been disclosed for $TIME. With a total supply of 1,000,000,000 tokens and Q1 circulating supply of 227.8M, investors cannot calculate market cap at listing, compare FDV to peers in the Web3 social/creator economy category, or apply the VDV model to assess realistic 12-month price dilution from unlock pressure. At a hypothetical $0.10 listing price, FDV would be $100M — reasonable for an early-stage ecosystem. At $0.50, FDV would be $500M — aggressive without proven revenue. Projects in the Web3 social/SocialFi category (Friend.tech, Stars Arena) have historically struggled to maintain valuations above $100M FDV without substantial active users. Publishing a clear listing price and FDV target ahead of TGE is essential for institutional participation and exchange due diligence.
■
Whitepaper Allocation ≠ Spreadsheet Allocation — Pre-Sale and KOL figures diverge across documents
▾
The published whitepaper states allocation as: Seed 20%, Pre-Sale 4%, Advisors 10%, Team 10%, Liquidity 9%, KOL Sale 5%, Community 22%, Ecosystem Growth 20%. However the tokenomics spreadsheet shows: Seed 15%, Pre-Sale 10%, KOL Sale 9%, Advisors 7.5%, Team 7.5%, Community 25%, Ecosystem 17% — with a confirmed total supply of 1,000,000,000. These are materially different documents. The whitepaper appears to be an earlier version not yet updated to reflect final tokenomics. This inconsistency is a credibility concern: investors who base decisions on the whitepaper are seeing different terms than those in the model. Coinstruct recommends the project immediately update the whitepaper to match the finalized spreadsheet allocations and version-control all tokenomics documents publicly.
■
Liquidity Fully Unlocked at TGE — Lock Duration Unknown — 90M tokens (9%) available day one, no lock confirmation
▾
The full 90M Liquidity allocation (9% of supply) is available from Q1 and remains flat throughout all 21 quarters, indicating it's provisioned as DEX/CEX liquidity from day one. This is structurally appropriate — liquidity needs to be available at launch. However, the whitepaper and spreadsheet do not specify the liquidity lock duration, the LP token custodian, or whether a portion is allocated to CEX market makers under specific lockup terms. Projects that fail to lock their liquidity pool tokens (even for 12 months) create rug-pull risk perception and are flagged by on-chain analytics tools. This needs explicit public confirmation: liquidity lock duration, platform (e.g. PinkLock, Team Finance), and the wallet address holding LP tokens.
■
No Staking APY, Burn, or Revenue Model Disclosed — Demand-side mechanics absent from documentation
▾
The whitepaper mentions staking "multiple tiers with variable APY" but provides zero specifics: no APY rates, no staking lockup periods, no minimum stake amounts, and no breakdown of how staking rewards are funded. It also references platform fees (all products use $TIME as payment) but does not state what percentage is burned versus redistributed versus retained as company revenue. Without these figures, it is impossible to model whether platform fee revenue can offset the 95.7% year-1 supply inflation. The creator economy and SocialFi sectors depend heavily on staking as a token sink to reduce sell pressure — Time of Heroes needs to publish specific staking mechanics before TGE to demonstrate that demand-side tokenomics can compete with the significant supply-side pressure from vesting unlocks.
✓
Team & Advisors Have Proper Cliffs — No team tokens at TGE; cliff-then-linear vesting structure
▾
One of the clearest strengths in the $TIME tokenomics is the vesting structure for Team and Advisors. Team tokens (7.5% of supply, 75M tokens) have a full 4-quarter (1-year) cliff — zero tokens at TGE — followed by linear unlock beginning Q5 and completing Q20, spanning approximately 4 years total. Advisors (7.5%, 75M tokens) have a 3-quarter cliff with linear unlock from Q4 through Q15. These are well-designed incentive structures that align team and advisor interests with long-term ecosystem success. The 4-year effective team vesting (including cliff) meets or exceeds institutional-grade standards. This signals that the core team is not designing for an exit — a meaningful positive signal relative to many comparable early-stage Web3 projects where team tokens begin unlocking within 12 months of launch.
✓
Multi-Product Ecosystem Creates Real Token Demand — $TIME is mandatory across 3 live/planned products
▾
TIME Universe has a genuine multi-product utility story: The Time of Heroes (celebrity time marketplace), MEEET (offline networking with NFT tiers), and TIME Network (decentralized SocialFi) all use $TIME as their exclusive payment currency. This means demand for $TIME is not speculative — it's tied to actual product usage: booking consultation sessions, purchasing NFT access passes, paying platform fees, and subscribing to premium content. If even one product gains meaningful traction (e.g. a successful Andrew Tate or Gary Vee partnership campaign for The Time of Heroes), it could generate real token velocity that partially offsets supply inflation. The go-to-market strategy of celebrity-anchored launches is high-risk, high-reward but has proven to drive rapid initial demand in comparable Web3 social platforms.
