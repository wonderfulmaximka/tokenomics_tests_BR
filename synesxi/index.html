INSIGHTS
▲
Year-1 Inflation of 135%: Public Presale + Investors Create Heavy Month-1 to Month-7 Sell Pressure: 282M non-liquidity tokens at TGE grows to 675M by month 12
▾
Excluding the 240M Liquidity/MM allocation (which is earmarked for market making, not selling), the circulating supply at TGE is approximately 282M tokens. By month 12, that grows to 675M, an increase of 393M tokens, representing 135% year-1 inflation. The primary drivers are: the Public Presale (441M tokens total, 50% at TGE with no cliff, then 5-month linear vest) and Private/Seed rounds (both have only 2-month cliffs before vesting begins). The Public Presale alone unlocks 220.6M tokens at TGE and then continues vesting 44.1M tokens/month from month 0 through month 5. Month 5 is the peak single-month unlock: all three investor tranches are simultaneously mid-vest, adding roughly 52M tokens in a single month. The project's price resilience in this window depends almost entirely on whether the platform launches with meaningful Solana ecosystem traction to absorb this supply expansion. Year 2 remains elevated at 62.5% inflation before normalizing in years 3-4.
▲
Public Presale Dominates the Raise at 88% of Total Funds: Concentrated Retail Sell Risk: $3M of $3.4M raised from public presale buyers at $0.0068, only 10% discount to listing
▾
The fundraising structure is unusual: of the $3.4M total raise, $3M (88.2%) comes from the Public Presale. The Seed Round raised only $100K and the Private Round $300K. This means the project's investor base is overwhelmingly public, retail-oriented buyers who paid $0.0068 per token against a listing price of $0.0075 (a 10% discount, implying a 1.10x at listing). These buyers have no cliff, 50% of their tokens are available at TGE and the remainder vests over 5 months. Retail presale investors with a 1.1x listing multiple and no cliff represent the highest dump risk of any allocation in this structure. Even a modest percentage of them deciding to take profit on day one at $0.0075 creates substantial sell pressure against the $1.8M of liquidity provisioned. The 441M presale tokens (22.06% of total supply) with a near-at-market entry price is the most structurally fragile element of the tokenomics.
▲
Month-13 Cliff Expiry: Team (240M) + Marketing (300M) Begin Vesting Simultaneously: 540M tokens begin unlocking from a single month, adding 22.5M tokens/month for 24 months
▾
Both the Team allocation (240M tokens, 12%) and the Marketing and Partnerships allocation (300M tokens, 15%) share identical vesting structures: 12-month cliff, 24-month linear vest. This means that at month 13 post-TGE (approximately December 2027 if TGE is November 2026), both allocations begin releasing simultaneously. The combined monthly unlock is 22.5M tokens per month (10M Team + 12.5M Marketing) for the full 24-month vesting window. While the 12-month cliff is appropriate and market-standard, the concurrent release of both the largest insider allocation and the largest operational allocation creates a structurally predictable sell overhang from month 13 onward. Sophisticated traders will likely anticipate this and position accordingly. The 18-month cliff on Treasury (299.5M tokens) adds a third wave beginning at month 19, though Treasury tokens are less likely to be immediately liquidated if properly governed. Staggering these cliffs by even 3 months each would meaningfully reduce the cliff-cliff effect.
■
$15M FDV is Conservative and Realistic: Among the Lowest in the Series: $3.9M initial MCAP at 26.1% float implies reasonable price discovery runway
▾
At $0.0075 per token with 2B total supply, the listing FDV is $15M and the initial market cap is approximately $3.9M (including liquidity) or $2.1M (excluding liquidity). These are exceptionally modest valuations for a Solana ecosystem project, comparable Solana marketplace and launchpad plays at comparable stages have listed at $30-100M FDV. The conservative FDV reduces the risk of post-listing FDV compression (where token price must fall to justify valuation relative to competitors) and provides genuine multiple expansion potential if the platform achieves even modest Solana ecosystem adoption. The Seed Round 3.95x multiple at listing is also modest, well below the 10-20x multiples seen in comparable early-stage crypto rounds. The implied total raise of $3.4M against a $15M FDV gives a 4.4x initial FDV/raise ratio, which is conservative and investor-friendly for the secondary market. The key risk is not overvaluation but whether the platform can launch by November 2026 as targeted.
■
2 Billion Token Supply Is Large in Absolute Terms, Creating Long Tail Inflation Through 2031: supply only reaches 100% by month 72 (November 2032), with Ecosystem vest running to 2031
▾
The 2,000,000,000 maximum supply is the largest absolute token count in the audited series, though absolute supply is less important than percentage allocation and vesting design. The Ecosystem bucket (280M tokens, 14%) has the longest vest at 60 months post a 2-month cliff, meaning ecosystem rewards continue distributing until approximately January 2032 at TGE of November 2026. While this is actually a positive design choice for long-term ecosystem health (avoiding a cliff at 24-36 months like most comparable projects), the practical consequence is that there is no "clean" fully-diluted endpoint until 6 years post-TGE. Token price will need to grow against continuous emission for the full 6-year window to produce meaningful returns for TGE buyers. The Treasury's 18-month cliff followed by 24-month vest (ending at month 42, approximately May 2030) is appropriate for a reserve allocation and signals the team is not planning short-term exits.
■
Platform Is Pre-Product With No Live Functionality Beyond a Waitlist: TGE target November 2026 gives 7 months to build a functional Solana marketplace and launch
▾
As of April 2026, the Synesxi website shows a pre-launch landing page with a waitlist registration form, feature descriptions, and navigation to blog/FAQ pages. There is no live product, no functional marketplace, no token discovery tool, no swap/bridge integration, no launchpad interface, and no developer onboarding portal. The product vision is broad: simultaneously targeting developers, traders, investors, service providers, and explorers on Solana with features including a swap/bridge, launchpad, incubator, talent pool, and rigorous vetting process. Each of these individually represents a significant development undertaking. With TGE targeted for November 2026 (approximately 7 months from audit), the team needs to deliver a minimum viable product across multiple of these features before or concurrent with token launch. The tokenomics are well-structured for this timeline, the 12-month cliffs on Team and Marketing provide a buffer, but execution risk is elevated given the scope of the platform vision and zero demonstrated product traction.
✓
Team and Treasury Vesting Are Well-Designed: 12m and 18m Cliffs Signal Long-Term Commitment: Team 0% at TGE, 12m cliff, 24m vest. Treasury 0% TGE, 18m cliff, 24m vest
▾
The Team allocation (240M tokens, 12%) has zero TGE unlock, a 12-month cliff, and 24-month linear vesting, meaning team members receive their first tokens in November 2027 at the earliest and are fully vested by November 2029. This is a strong alignment signal: team members have no incentive to exit before at least a year of platform operation. The Treasury (299.5M tokens, 15%) has an 18-month cliff, meaning no Treasury tokens are available until May 2028, a year and a half after TGE. This is the longest cliff in the structure and provides meaningful protocol protection during the most volatile post-listing period. Together these two allocations represent 27% of total supply (539.5M tokens) that is fully locked for 12-18 months post-TGE. Combined with the Marketing allocation (also 12m cliff, same vest), the total supply locked for a minimum of 12 months post-TGE is 42%, providing structural protection against insider dumps during the initial growth phase.
✓
Investor Multiples Are Compressed and Investor-Friendly for Secondary Buyers: Seed 3.95x, Private 1.67x, Presale 1.10x, no 10-50x VC multiples that typically destroy retail
▾
The fundraising structure avoids the most common tokenomics failure mode: giving insiders 10-50x listing multiples that create overwhelming incentives to dump on retail. The Seed Round at $0.0019 gives a 3.95x at listing, meaningful but not egregious. Private at $0.0045 gives 1.67x, barely above water. Public Presale at $0.0068 gives 1.10x, essentially breakeven. This compression means all three investor groups have limited profit-taking incentive relative to many comparable projects. The two-month cliff on Seed and Private (rather than zero cliff) provides a small but meaningful delay before selling begins. The total raise of $3.4M is also lean: the project raised enough to fund development without overselling tokens to insiders. The main concern is not insider multiples but the sheer volume of presale tokens (441M, 22.06% of supply) and their immediate availability, which creates collective retail sell pressure rather than institutional sell pressure.
