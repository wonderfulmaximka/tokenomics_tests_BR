INSIGHTS
▲
56.56% of Total Supply Already Circulating on Exchanges at TGE: no cliff, no vesting, no lock, the largest single allocation is instantly liquid
▾
The "Public & Community Distribution" allocation of 56,560,000 IGSC (56.56% of total supply) is described explicitly as "already circulating on exchanges." This means there is no token generation event in the traditional sense, more than half of the entire supply is already tradeable with zero vesting, zero cliff, and zero lock-up. This is the single most severe structural flaw in the tokenomics. It means any holder of the public allocation can sell at any time with no restriction. Combined with a low-utility, pre-launch platform with no disclosed traction metrics, the sell propensity for this tranche is extremely high. For context, comparable Web3 messaging projects at launch have initial floats of 5-20%. A 56.6% float is not a launch, it is a token that was distributed before the product existed, seeking a post-hoc narrative. This structure makes sustained price appreciation dependent entirely on new buyer demand absorbing constant sell pressure from 56.5M unrestricted tokens.
▲
No Listing Price, No FDV, No Raise Amount Disclosed: critical financial parameters missing from the whitepaper entirely
▾
The whitepaper contains no listing price, no FDV, no total capital raised, and no round prices for any investor allocation. This is a fundamental disclosure failure. Without these figures it is impossible to calculate: investor ROI multiples at listing, the actual market capitalization at launch, how much capital the project has to fund development, or whether insiders are in significant profit at any given price point. The whitepaper also obscures the total supply, the PDF renders it as ",," with decimals listed as "3", suggesting either a redaction of a large supply number or a typesetting error that was never corrected before publication. In either case, publishing a whitepaper with core financial parameters missing or illegible is a material transparency failure. Investors cannot price risk without these numbers.
▲
Strategic Pivot From VR/AR Casino Gaming Destroys Token Continuity: IGSC was designed for gambling; TapTap Messenger repurposes it as a messaging currency
▾
IGSC was originally designed as a VR/AR casino gaming token. The whitepaper openly acknowledges this pivot to Web3 messaging, framing it as "recognizing growing demand for decentralized communication." However, this pivot raises serious legitimacy questions. Token holders who purchased IGSC under the original gaming thesis now hold a token for an entirely different use case. The original tokenomics, smart contract design, and utility mechanisms were built for gambling, not messaging. There is no disclosure of whether existing token holders consented to this pivot, whether the smart contracts were redeployed, or what happened to the original gaming platform and its investors. Furthermore, pivoting from a casino gaming token, a sector with well-known regulatory exposure, to a "privacy-focused messaging platform" suggests the gaming model may have encountered regulatory or market difficulties rather than being a proactive strategic choice.
▲
Insider Allocation 43.44% With Discretionary Development Vesting: Development tokens vest "based on roadmap progress" giving team unilateral release control
▾
The insider allocation totals 43,440,000 IGSC (43.44%): Team & Advisors 20%, Development & Operations 15%, Strategic Partnerships 5%, Reserve 3.44%. This is a high insider concentration, but the more serious concern is the Development allocation's vesting trigger. The whitepaper states Development tokens vest "quarterly releases based on roadmap progress", meaning the team can determine what constitutes "roadmap progress" and release tokens accordingly. This is not a trustless, on-chain schedule. It is a discretionary mechanism that allows insiders to self-certify milestones and accelerate token releases. Combined with the Reserve (3.44%, 24m cliff, 60m linear) which is described as available for "emergency fund" and "market stabilization" without defined parameters, there are at least two insider allocations totalling 18.44% that have subjective, team-controlled release conditions.
▲
Whitepaper Contains Direct Contradictions on Blockchain Infrastructure: simultaneously claims Ethereum as primary chain and BEP-20 / BSC deployment
▾
The whitepaper states under Technical Architecture: "Primary Blockchain: Ethereum" and "Smart Contracts: BEP-20 token with additional functionality." Under Token Overview it states "Blockchain: Binance Smart Chain (BEP-)" and describes "BEP-20 standard implementation." These are contradictory claims, a BEP-20 token is a BSC token, not an Ethereum token. The whitepaper then describes Layer 2 solutions including "Polygon Integration" and "Optimistic Rollups" which are Ethereum ecosystem features, not BSC features. Additionally, Paragraph 11 reads "Core Contracts: IGSC Token Contract, BEP- standard implementation", the standard number is truncated, suggesting BEP-20 was not fully formatted. For a technical whitepaper from a team claiming to include a CTO with blockchain architecture expertise, these contradictions suggest the document was assembled hastily or from template without thorough technical review. Investors cannot determine which blockchain actually hosts the token from reading the whitepaper.
▲
Unverifiable Partnership Claims With Major Institutions: claims MIT, Stanford, Binance listing "in progress," CertiK audit, Chainlink, Stripe, MoonPay
▾
The whitepaper lists an extensive roster of partnerships across three pages that includes MIT Digital Currency Initiative, Stanford Blockchain Research Center, CertiK (security auditing), Chainlink (oracle services), Stripe (fiat on-ramp), MoonPay ("integration completed Q4 2024"), Binance ("listing negotiations in progress"), Coinbase ("technical integration discussions"), Kraken ("partnership agreement signed"), and a Twitch Streamers Alliance. None of these partnerships are independently verifiable from the whitepaper, there are no contract references, no press releases cited, no on-chain evidence. The CertiK audit is described as "ongoing" without a published audit report link. The Binance listing claim is particularly notable: Binance has a rigorous listing process and claiming negotiations are "in progress" without evidence is a common practice in low-quality projects to create false legitimacy. The breadth and prestige of these claimed partnerships, combined with the complete absence of supporting evidence, significantly increases the credibility risk of this project.
■
Year-2 Inflation 20.5%: Manageable But Concentrated in Team Unlock: 20M team tokens begin releasing from month 13, adding 416K tokens/month
▾
Given the 56.6% float already in circulation, the year-1 inflation is only 7.7% (4.375M new tokens from Strategic and Development vesting). This is low in absolute terms. However, year 2 brings 20.5% inflation as Team tokens begin their 48-month linear vesting from month 13 (416,667 tokens/month), and Development accelerates to quarterly releases. The combined supply grows from 60.9M to 73.4M tokens in year 2. The year-2 inflation is where the structural problem compounds: Team tokens unlock when the project is still in early growth phase (roadmap targets only 100,000 users by Q2 2025 and 1M users by Q4 2025, aggressive targets for an unproven pivot project). If TapTap Messenger has not achieved meaningful user traction by month 13, Team token unlocks arrive when sell pressure from the public float is already elevated. Inflation stabilises by year 5-6 at around 6% annually, and the total supply is fixed at 100M, which at minimum prevents hyperinflationary emission.
■
Token Utility Exists on Paper But Is Undeployed: 9 utility vectors described, but TapTap Messenger has no confirmed active user base
▾
The whitepaper describes 9 $IGSC utility vectors: P2P payments, creator tips and subscriptions, revenue sharing, governance voting, staking rewards, liquidity mining, referral bonuses, activity rewards, and merchant payments. These are legitimate and well-designed utility categories for a messaging platform. The burn mechanism (0.1% of all platform transactions + 2% of creator earnings quarterly) would create genuine deflationary pressure at scale. However, all of these utilities are contingent on a live, active user base, and the whitepaper provides no traction data whatsoever: no active user count, no message volume, no creator count, no transaction volume. The roadmap marks Phase 1 as "In Progress" as of September 2024 with a target of 10,000 beta users, and the CEO's listed achievement of "driving user growth to over 100,000 active users" is unverifiable from the document. Until the platform demonstrates real usage, all utility vectors are theoretical and the token has no organic demand driver beyond speculation.
