INSIGHTS
✓
69% Mining Allocation Is the Most Community-Oriented Distribution in This Audit Series: 254.6M tokens distributed via Proof-of-Utility to node operators, miners, and contributors
▾
The allocation of 69% of total supply to mining and node rewards is exceptional by any standard. Industry benchmarks show community/ecosystem allocations averaging 35-40% for DePIN and AI infrastructure projects. Drive369's 69% allocation, 254,610,000 DRIVE, is the single largest community-facing bucket seen across all projects audited in this series. Combined with Alpha Miners (3.69%), total community-accessible supply via participation reaches 72.7%. The team receives only 6.9%, well below the industry average of 17-20%, and with confirmed 3-year linear vesting. This structure creates strong alignment: the vast majority of tokens can only be obtained by doing real work on the network (running nodes, contributing compute, training federated models), not by buying private rounds or holding pre-mined allocations. For node operators and early participants, this design is one of the most favorable seen in a DePIN project.
▲
Mining Emission Schedule Is Undisclosed: 254.6M Tokens With No Release Rate: without a schedule, year-1 inflation could range from 67% to 155% depending on duration
▾
The 69% mining allocation is the dominant structural feature, but it creates the project's most significant unresolved risk: there is no disclosed emission schedule. The website states browser mining is live in Q1 2026 and rewards are already being distributed, but no annual emission rate, halving schedule, or total emission timeline is published anywhere. Modeling the impact requires assumptions. If the 254.6M tokens emit over 3 years, year-1 inflation from mining alone is approximately 155% relative to the TGE circulating supply. Over 5 years, it is 93%. Over 7 years, 67%. Each of these scenarios creates materially different price dynamics. A fast emission schedule rewards early participants richly but creates severe sell pressure for token buyers; a slow schedule is more price-supportive but less motivating for node operators. The roadmap target of 1,000+ active mining nodes by Q4 2026 suggests meaningful near-term emission. Until the emission rate is published as a formal schedule, this is the most critical missing data point in the entire tokenomics.
▲
$6M Strategic Round Has No Corresponding Token Allocation Bucket: five-bucket allocation table accounts for 100% of supply with no investor line
▾
The website's allocation table (Mining 69%, Liquidity 11.11%, DAO Treasury 9.3%, Team 6.9%, Alpha Miners 3.69%) accounts for exactly 100% of the 369M supply. There is no investor, strategic, or private round allocation bucket. Yet Drive369 just closed a $6M strategic round. Four interpretations exist. First, the $6M was structured as a pure SAFE or equity instrument with no token warrant, meaning investors hold equity in the legal entity, not tokens. Second, investors will receive tokens from the DAO Treasury allocation (9.3%), which has no disclosed vesting schedule. Third, the allocation table on the website is outdated and will be revised to include an investor bucket when tokenomics are finalized. Fourth, investors purchased $DRIVE through OTC secondary market from existing holders. Option 1 is the most investor-aligned but would be unusual for a $6M crypto round. Option 2 would dilute the DAO Treasury's governance function. Option 3 means the current table cannot be relied upon. The project needs to clarify this explicitly before TGE.
▲
No Listing Price or FDV Makes Valuation Modeling Impossible: no public pricing data exists for $DRIVE despite live browser mining and confirmed $6M raise
▾
Despite browser mining being live (Q1 2026 active per roadmap), a confirmed $6M raise, and an agent staking contract deployed on Base, there is no disclosed listing price, implied FDV, or round price anywhere in any document. Without a listing price, it is impossible to calculate: what the implied market cap is at TGE, whether the 11.1% liquidity allocation is sufficient relative to FDV, what the implied multiple is for the $6M investors, and whether the mining reward rate is economically rational relative to token price. Comparably positioned DePIN AI projects at this stage (Render, Akash, Nosana) listed at FDVs ranging from $30M to $250M. Drive369's positioning between edge AI, agent OS, and DePIN storage is genuinely differentiated, but without price data, no risk-adjusted return modeling is possible for any participant.
■
DAO Treasury 9.3% and Alpha Miners 3.7% Have No Disclosed Vesting: 47.9M tokens across two buckets with no lock-up or distribution schedule
▾
The DAO Treasury (34.3M tokens, 9.3%) and Alpha Miners (13.6M tokens, 3.69%) together represent 47.9M tokens, 13% of total supply, for which no cliff, vesting period, or distribution timeline is disclosed. The DAO Treasury is described as governing protocol upgrades, treasury allocation, and strategic partnerships; it presumably requires DAO votes to release, which provides some governance-based lock-up protection. Alpha Miners tokens are described as rewards for early community contributors, but no distribution schedule or lock-up period is specified. If both pools were fully liquid at TGE alongside the Liquidity allocation, the effective circulating supply could be 54.6M to 102.5M tokens on day one, producing a very wide range of possible initial market caps. The Liquidity allocation alone (11.1%, 41M tokens) as the TGE float would be clean. The uncertainty about DAO Treasury and Alpha Miners timing is a key variable.
■
Browser Mining Is Live But Reward Economics Are Unproven: site shows 42 $DRIVE/round with 156 active nodes, but no model for sustainable reward rate
▾
The website's federated learning section shows live metrics: 2,847 training rounds completed, 156 active nodes, 42 $DRIVE per round in rewards, and 8.2 TF total compute contributed. This confirms the browser mining product is genuinely active. However, 42 DRIVE per round across 156 nodes implies significant per-node daily issuance at current network scale. Without knowing the listing price, it's impossible to assess whether current reward rates are economically sustainable at scale. At $0.01/DRIVE, 42 tokens per round is $0.42, negligible. At $0.10/DRIVE, it's $4.20 per round, which becomes economically meaningful for node operators. The reward rate calibration, which should be tied to the emission schedule, is the key variable that determines whether the mining program creates organic demand or just constant sell pressure from miners immediately liquidating rewards. The roadmap targets 1,000+ mining nodes by Q4 2026, which would require distributing the same reward pool across 6x more participants, halving individual payouts unless the pool expands.

Team Allocation 6.9% With 3-Year Vesting Is Among the Lowest and Best-Structured: half the industry average, fully vested over 36 months with no large immediate unlock
▾
The Team allocation of 6.9% (25,461,000 DRIVE) with confirmed 3-year vesting is a strong signal of founder alignment. Industry benchmarks show team allocations averaging 17-20% for AI and DePIN projects, with a recent trend toward higher team percentages in bull markets. Drive369's 6.9% is roughly one-third of the industry average. Combined with 3-year linear vesting (no disclosed cliff beyond the vesting label on the website), team tokens vest at approximately 707,000 DRIVE per month, at any plausible listing price, this creates manageable monthly team unlock pressure relative to the total supply. The low team allocation also means that even at a modest listing FDV of $40-50M, team members are not sitting on disproportionate paper wealth that creates an exit incentive early. This is one of the most clearly investor-aligned elements of the tokenomics structure.
